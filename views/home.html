Access

An accessibility template focusing on the four outlined areas of disability affecting web browsing: visual, hearing, motor, and cognitive.

The Access template's purpose is to provide a solid foundation from which to construct the front-end of a web site in as close adherance as possible to WCAG 2 compliance.

Access is a constant work-in-progress, and reminds those utilizing this resource that it is simply a guide intended to aid developers, and to always check their level of compliance with the appropriate institution.

Template Overview

Visual The visual aspect of accessibility largely relies on screen reader compatibility, which is controlled via keyboard functionality.

Hearing The hearing aspect of accessibility is focused on audio-enabled media alternatives such as transcripts for videos, and ensuring the UX does not rely on any audio-based cues.

Motor The motor aspect of accessibility is greatly aided by ease-of-access, generally via keyboard functionality that is manipulated by assistive technologies.

Cognitive The cognitive aspect of accessibility is tied to the organization and understandability of pages. Media that assists in content comprehension is of great help to individuals with cognitive challenges.
