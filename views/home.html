Access

An accessibility endeavor focusing on the four outlined areas of disability affecting web use: visual, hearing, motor, and cognitive.

This project's purpose is to spread awareness of accessibility concerns regarding internet use by disabled individuals, as well as to provide a condensed source of information that helps in bringing web sites as close in adherance as possible to WCAG 2 compliance.

Access is a tool to educate oneself about accessible HTML application & technique. By laying out our markup with accessibility as a guide, we positively affect other areas of front-end development and broaden our view of the web as a whole.

Accessibility Overview

Visual

The visual aspect of accessibility is all about screen reader compatibility, which is controlled via keyboard functionality. Ensuring proper semantic markup is key in aiding visually-impaired users.

Hearing

The hearing aspect of accessibility is focused on audio-enabled media alternatives such as transcripts for videos, and ensuring the UI does not rely on audio-based cues or instructions.

Motor

The motor aspect of accessibility is greatly aided by ease-of-access, generally via keyboard functionality that is manipulated by assistive technologies.

Cognitive The cognitive aspect of accessibility is tied to the organization and understandability of pages. Media that assists in content comprehension is of great help to individuals facing cognitive challenges.
